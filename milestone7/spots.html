Hangout Spots

No plan? No problem. Here's where to go when you just want somewhere to exist with other humans.

😌 Chill Spots

Low-key places where you can relax, talk, and actually hear each other without yelling over a bass drop.

The Night Owl Bar

A neighborhood bar with a chill vibe, great prices, and trivia on Wednesdays. Not the kind of place that blasts music at conversation-ending volumes. Grab a drink, find a booth, and stay as long as you want. Student-friendly and never pretentious.

$$ 🦉 Bar

Campus Corner Cafe

Open until midnight and genuinely student-friendly — meaning they don't give you the look when you've been sitting for two hours with one drink. Good music, good coffee, and a crowd that's either studying or pretending to. Either way, solid vibes.

$ ☕ Cafe

Pioneer Park

A large city park with open grass areas, walking paths, and scenic views that are genuinely worth the walk. Great in the late afternoon or early evening before it gets dark. Free, obviously — it's a park. Bring food, a speaker, and a frisbee if you want the full experience.

Free 🌳 Outdoor

👯 Group Hangouts

When you need to entertain a group of people with different moods, these spots handle it.

Strike Zone Bowling

Six lanes, a snack bar, and glow-in-the-dark bowling on weekends. Weeknight prices drop significantly — sometimes as low as $2 per game with shoe rental included. Great for groups of 4–8 who want something competitive but not intense. No skill required, which is kind of the point.

$2–$6/game 🎳 Bowling

Level Up Arcade

A full-size arcade with classic machines, modern redemption games, air hockey, and a dedicated Street Fighter cabinet that someone has definitely spent too much money on. Buy a card upfront and share it with your group. Hours run late on weekends — open until 1am Friday and Saturday.

$10–$20 🕹️ Arcade

Sweet Cravings Dessert Bar

After-dinner dessert spots are criminally underrated as hangout destinations. This place does elaborate milkshakes, loaded waffles, and build-your-own ice cream. Open late, never crowded enough to be uncomfortable, and impossible to leave without taking at least one photo.

$8–$14 🍦 Dessert

🌄 Outdoor Ideas

St. George has scenery most cities would kill for. Get outside and actually use it.

Red Cliffs Desert Reserve

Trails through stunning red rock scenery just outside the city. Multiple difficulty levels make it accessible for any group. Sunset hikes are particularly worth it — bring water, charge your phone for photos, and go before dark.

⭐ 4.9  •  Free  •  Sunrise to Sunset

🥾 Hiking

Sunset Overlook Point

A local viewpoint accessible by short drive and a 10-minute walk. The view of the valley at golden hour is legitimately impressive. Popular with couples and friend groups alike. Bring a blanket for the golden hour and leave before it gets too dark to safely hike back.

⭐ 4.7  •  Free  •  Best at 7–8pm

🌅 Scenic

📍 Spot Tips

  • For outdoor spots, always check sunrise/sunset times — trails close at dark in most parks for safety reasons.
  • Bring water even on short outdoor outings. St. George weather can be surprisingly intense, especially in warmer months.
  • Arcades and bowling alleys book up fast on weekends after 9pm — call ahead or show up before 8pm to guarantee a spot.
  • Many hangout spots near campus are walking or biking distance — skip the parking hassle when you can.
