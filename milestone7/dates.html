Date Ideas

Low pressure, actually affordable, and way better than "I don't know, what do YOU want to do?"

☕ Low-Pressure First Date Ideas

First dates work best when there's something to do besides stare at each other. Here are situations that naturally keep conversation flowing.

☕

Coffee Walk

Grab coffee from a campus café and walk somewhere scenic. Walking side-by-side takes some of the social pressure off direct eye contact, and it gives you something to comment on as you go. Low cost, easy to extend if it's going well, easy to wrap up if it's not. The golden hour walk around Pioneer Park pairs perfectly with this.

Under $10 💚 Low Pressure
🍦

Dessert Run

Meeting for dessert instead of a full dinner is one of the most underrated first date formats. It's shorter, lower stakes, and nobody is sitting awkwardly through a $60 meal hoping the vibe gets better. Go to Sweet Cravings or a nearby ice cream spot, share something ridiculous, and just talk. Simple and effective.

$8–$15 💚 Low Pressure
⛳

Mini Golf

Mini golf at Fiesta Fun is one of the best low-pressure date activities available. There's always something happening on the course to talk about, a little friendly competition keeps the energy up, and terrible golf shots are endearing rather than embarrassing. Costs under $10, takes about 45 minutes, and goes well with food after.

$7–$10 🎯 Activity
🎬

Campus Movie Night

The student union's free outdoor movie screenings are an easy win for a casual first date. It's free, there's no awkward restaurant silence to navigate, and sitting outside watching a film together is naturally comfortable. Bring a blanket, grab snacks beforehand, and show up with a backup plan for after (dessert, walk, etc.).

Free 💚 Free

💵 Budget Date Ideas

Spending money isn't what makes a date good. These prove it.

🧺

Picnic at the Park

Grab food from a grocery store or grab-and-go spot, find a blanket, and head to Pioneer Park or the Red Cliffs area for a picnic. The setup costs next to nothing and the effort reads as thoughtful. Sunset timing makes this especially good — showing up around 6–7pm for the golden hour is hard to beat. Total cost for two people rarely tops $20.

Under $20 🌿 Outdoor
🌅

Sunset Overlook Drive

Sometimes the date is just driving somewhere with a good view and watching the sunset together. The overlook point near the edge of the city has a genuinely beautiful panoramic view of the valley. Pack something small to drink, put on a good playlist, and park up there for 30–40 minutes around golden hour. Free, memorable, and surprisingly romantic.

Free 💚 Free

🎉 Fun Date Ideas

More energy, more laughs, more stories to tell afterward.

🎳

Bowling Night

Glow bowling on weekends at Strike Zone is an easy date win. Low skill requirement means neither person is at a disadvantage, the competitive edge keeps the energy up, and renting shoes together for some reason always breaks the ice. Grab a soda at the snack bar and make it a little tournament. Best of 3 games, loser buys dessert.

$10–$20 total 🎯 Activity
🎧

Live Music Night

Indie Night Live on Fridays gives you a shared experience to react to together, which is the whole point of a good date. Standing next to someone at a live show and feeling the same song hit at the same time is genuinely a bonding experience. Student discount brings the door price down to $5. Grab dinner beforehand at one of the cheap eats spots nearby and make it a full evening.

$5–$15 🎸 Event

💡 Date Night Tips

  • Have a backup plan. If one idea falls through — rained out picnic, sold-out show — knowing where to go next makes you look prepared instead of panicked.
  • Walking dates work well when the pressure of sitting and staring across a table feels like too much. Movement makes conversation easier.
  • Cheap doesn't mean lazy. A thoughtfully planned free date beats an expensive generic dinner every time. It's the effort that reads.
  • Activities that involve mild competition (mini golf, bowling, arcade) are consistently rated more fun than purely passive dates by both people — there's something to react to and talk about.
  • Food trucks make surprisingly great casual date spots — low commitment, great variety, and the standing-around-eating vibe is naturally relaxed.
